MY PROJECT

NOMAD
BALANCE

Focus Timer • Fasting Tracker • Active Breaks • Task Planner

If you sit at a desk all day, you know the drill: hours fly by, your back aches, you forget to eat properly, and your to-do list is a mess. I built NomadBalance to fix all of that — in one app, with zero friction.

01. The Problem

We spend 8+ hours a day staring at screens. Productivity apps help you work more, but nobody helps you work better. There's no single tool that handles focus, nutrition timing, physical movement, and task planning together.

So you end up juggling a Pomodoro app, a fasting tracker, a stretch reminder, and a to-do list — four apps that don't talk to each other and constantly fight for your attention.

The desk worker's daily reality:
- Focus drain: Constant context-switching kills deep work.
- Forgotten meals: You eat too late, too early, or skip entirely.
- Zero movement: 6 hours sitting without a single stretch.
- Task chaos: Priorities scattered across sticky notes and apps.

02. Four Pillars, One App

NomadBalance organizes your entire workday around four areas that matter most. Free, no install needed, works offline as a PWA.

FLOW
Focus Timer
Structured work/break cycles with binaural audio (40 Hz deep-focus, 10 Hz relaxation).
FUEL
Intermittent Fasting
Set your eating window (16/8, 14/10, custom). Automatic meal timing synced to your schedule.
BODY
Active Breaks
27 exercises across 5 muscle groups, designed specifically for desk workers.
JOURNAL
Task Planner
Daily priorities, drag-to-reorder, time alarms, auto-carryover. Full history with search.

03. A Typical Day

NomadBalance works best when you follow the rhythm. Hit "Start Day" in the morning — a wizard sets up your eating window, focus blocks, exercise plan, and tasks. Then the app guides you through the day:

The Daily Flow

Focus blocks — timer counts down with ambient audio. Break notification when it ends.
Exercise breaks — aligned with your break windows. Image, reps, form tips. 2-3 minutes.
Meal windows — the app knows when to eat and tracks your intake.
Day close — guided breathing session (4-7-8 or Box Breathing), stats review, sign off.

04. Why I Built This

As someone who works remotely and spends long hours at a desk, I got tired of duct-taping together five different apps to manage my day. I wanted one tool that understood the connection between focus, nutrition, movement, and planning — because they're not separate problems; they're the same problem.

NomadBalance is free, runs in the browser, works offline, and needs no installation. It's a PWA you can add to your home screen and use like a native app.

ROOTS &
HORIZONS

Remote Work • Sardinia • Global Reach

The world is moving at light speed. We consume "Fast Content", chase quarterly targets, and adapt to AI acceleration daily. In this high-velocity environment, the ultimate luxury isn't just "time", but "grounding".

Torre delle Stelle: Where nature is the office backdrop.

I chose to base my life in Sardinia not to escape the race, but to run it better. Living away from the chaotic metropolis allows for a "Deep Work" state that is hard to achieve elsewhere. It's about contrasting the digital frenzy with analog peace: family, silence, and the sea.

But to make this sustainable, you need infrastructure. And the reason this lifestyle works is a small, strategic asset: Cagliari-Elmas Airport.

01. The Praise of the "Small Airport"

The Anti-Hub Experience:
- No Queues: Security takes minutes, not hours.
- Human Scale: From car park to gate in a heartbeat.
- Efficiency: One of the best-connected airports in Europe relative to its "livability".

Nature & Ambition

PUBLISHING
vs AI

Programmatic Crisis • Zero-Click Future • The New Revenue Models

The Problem: We are moving from the "Open Web" (Human traffic visiting sites) to a "Closed LLM" ecosystem (AI synthesizing answers). As a digital publisher or programmatic expert, I have to ask: who pays the bill when the user stops clicking?

Visualizing the shift from Distributed Web to Centralized Model.

01. The Consumer Dilemma

"Is it really fair, even if it's more efficient?" This is the fundamental ethical question.

The Cost of Convenience:
- The Illusion: We love removing friction.
- The Cost: Loss of serendipity and critical thinking.
- The Black Box: Advice is indistinguishable from advertising.

Conclusion: Convenience almost always beats privacy, unless the consumer is actively educated.

02. System Equilibrium

Parasite or Symbiosis?

The "Model Collapse" Risk: If Publishers become penny-paid suppliers, they stop producing quality. The AI then feeds on its own synthetic content.

The Correction: We either get massive licensing deals or "Data Walls".

DIY PROJECT

THE MUSIC
SETUP

Zero Clutter • Custom Joinery • High Fidelity

The Problem: A living room invaded by cables, tripods, and bulky desks. The need for a professional DJ setup that disappears when the music stops.

01. The Dilemma

My current setup sounds great but looks like a backstage area. Tripods take up floor space, and cables are everywhere.

Project Requirements:
- NO TRIPODS (Clean floor)
- Retractable: Must fit in tight corners.
- Cable Void: Zero visible wiring.
- Integrated Audio: Speakers on the deck, not on the floor.

02. The "Invisible" Console

The Cable Management Gap

Instead of fighting with cables, I designed a dedicated "void". A 10cm gap between the main deck and the vinyl storage allows power and RCA cables to drop straight down, unseen.

The Result: A floating effect where the gear seems to sit on a clean block of wood, with no visual noise.

MACRO THESIS

THE MACRO
BET

Capital Rotation • Hard Assets • The Super-Cycle

The biggest wealth transfer of our decade is happening right now. We are moving from the era of "Asset-Light Software" (SaaS, Apps) to the era of "Asset-Heavy Infrastructure." The question for the smart investor isn't "which app will win?", but "who owns the physical value chain required to power them all?"

Visualizing the bottleneck: Capital vs Constraints.

01. The "CapEx" Tsunami

Right now, the "Hyperscalers" (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) are engaged in an unprecedented arms race, pouring hundreds of billions into physical assets.

The Investment Reality:
- Physical Lag: Software scales instantly; Power Plants take 10 years.
- Grid Saturation: You can buy the chips, but you can't plug them in.
- Sovereignty: Nations are treating data centers as critical military infrastructure.

02. Investment Outcomes

A. Nuclear Renaissance

The "Baseload" Crisis: Renewables are too intermittent for 24/7 industrial demand.

Outcome: Tech giants become energy companies (MSFT + Three Mile Island).

WINNER: URANIUM

B. Sovereign Protectionism

Data Nationalism: Every nation wants its own sovereign intelligence to avoid dependence.

Outcome: Fragmentation of the global internet into walled supply chains.

WINNER: DEFENSE

C. Physical Constraints

Heat & Power: Traditional air cooling fails. Grids are old.

Outcome: Massive shift to Liquid Cooling and Copper super-cycle for grids.

WINNER: COPPER
